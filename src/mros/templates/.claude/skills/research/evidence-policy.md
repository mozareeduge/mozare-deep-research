# Terms, evidence, claims, and counterevidence

## Exact wording and term records

When the user asks for exact terms, wording, definitions, or concepts introduced by references:

1. locate a candidate passage;
2. reopen the bounded source page or section;
3. preserve a short uninterrupted quotation unless omissions are explicitly marked;
4. record source identity, location, access/edition limits, and verification method;
5. create an evidence record;
6. create a term record linked to that verified evidence;
7. keep the source's wording separate from interpretation.

Conversation memory, a search snippet, an abstract, or an earlier model summary is not verification.

A term record states who used which wording, where, and how it functions. It does not claim that the author coined the term unless the historical evidence supports coinage.

## Evidence packets

For consequential evidence preserve:

- exact quotation or controlled paraphrase;
- bounded location;
- local context where needed;
- proposition supported;
- role: define, support, qualify, refute, contextualize, or example;
- verification method;
- access and interpretation limits.

## Claims

A central claim must identify supporting evidence, scope, uncertainty, and credible qualification or counterevidence. Use statuses:

- supported;
- contested;
- qualified;
- insufficient;
- unresolved.

Do not merge different authors' vocabularies into a synthetic definition without showing the transition. Do not turn structural difficulty into intentional refusal, association into causation, analogy into identity, or a source example into a general thesis.

## Counter-search

Before settling central claims, test:

- alternate chronology or attribution;
- different definitions or uses of the term;
- critical and marginalized perspectives;
- common cultural availability versus specific influence;
- dependence among sources;
- translation, edition, access, or metadata problems;
- a simpler explanation.

A failed or weakened claim remains useful as an explicit limit or research gap.
