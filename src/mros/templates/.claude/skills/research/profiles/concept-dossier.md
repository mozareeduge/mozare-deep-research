# Concept-dossier profile

Use this profile when the user asks for a research note or dossier around a concept and emphasizes exact terms, wording, definitions, or concepts introduced and used by references.

## Internal work order

Infer these coverage slots rather than asking the user to fill a form:

1. naming and earliest explicit uses;
2. canonical or consolidating primary texts;
3. exact terms, definitions, distinctions, methods, and slogans used by each key reference;
4. precursors and adjacent practices, kept distinct from retrospective absorption;
5. scholarly interpretation and conceptual placement;
6. criticism, counterpositions, exclusions, and public or institutional disputes where relevant;
7. later reception, pedagogy, and transformations;
8. unresolved chronology, access, or attribution questions.

## Source-role map

Seek the strongest available set across these roles:

- `primary-core`;
- `primary-adjacent`;
- `scholarly-interpretation`;
- `critique`;
- `reception`;
- `context`.

The roles are coverage checks, not quotas. Record a gap when a role is unavailable or irrelevant.

## Search sequence

1. exact concept phrase and spelling variants;
2. named canonical authors and works;
3. publisher, author, archive, syllabus, or institutional primary pages;
4. backward and forward citation chains;
5. precursor and adjacent terms;
6. criticism and counterposition searches;
7. later reception and pedagogy;
8. non-English variants only when substantively useful.

Run an initial source map, then a targeted gap search. Avoid repeated broad searching after the same source clusters recur.

## Reading and term extraction

For each core source, create a compact note under `source-notes/` that records:

- the source's role and argument movement;
- exact terms it introduces or makes consequential;
- short uninterrupted quotations with bounded locations;
- how the term functions: coinage, definition, distinction, method, slogan, critique, reception label, or variant;
- what is source wording and what is the researcher's interpretation;
- access and edition limits;
- relations to other references.

Append one `term` record per consequential formulation. A term is not verified unless its linked evidence contains an exact quotation reopened from a bounded source location.

## Synthesis order

Plan the dossier only after term and evidence records exist. A useful default is:

1. concise orientation and scope;
2. chronology or formation map;
3. reference-by-reference term notes;
4. cross-reference conceptual relations;
5. disputes and counterpositions;
6. later reception or transformation;
7. unresolved questions and source limits;
8. full references;
9. a compact visualization when it clarifies chronology, relation, or terminology.

Do not smooth different authors' vocabularies into one definition. Preserve who used which term, where, and for what operation.
