# Output composition and audit

Match the artifact to the user's request. Do not force every inquiry into a generic report.

## Concept or research-note dossier

A term-centered dossier normally contains:

1. concise scope and orientation;
2. a chronology or formation map;
3. reference-by-reference notes showing exact terms and wording;
4. an analytical map of relations among those formulations;
5. precursors and adjacent practices kept distinct from the named concept;
6. disputes, criticism, and limitations;
7. later reception or transformation;
8. unresolved questions and access limits;
9. full references;
10. a simple visualization when it clarifies chronology, terms, or relations.

Write from evidence records, not the search transcript. Introduce concepts through what they are and how they operate before contrasting them. Keep prose clear enough that the reader does not need the repository's internal method.

## Audit

Before completion, check:

- central claims resolve to verified evidence;
- exact terms resolve to verified exact quotations;
- citations identify the source actually consulted;
- primary and secondary materials are not confused;
- indirect quotations and inaccessible sources are marked;
- important criticism and disagreement are represented;
- source dependence is not mistaken for independent confirmation;
- the dossier does not claim completeness beyond the search;
- generated text is never presented as source evidence;
- the deliverable answers the requested genre and emphasis.

Write `audit.yaml` as `pass`, `pass_with_limits`, or `fail`. Repair blocking failures before delivery; preserve non-blocking limits in the final note.
